Our Mission

Who is The Phlebotomy Trainer?

During a gap year from university, I joined the NHS seeking real-world experience, not anticipating that it would ignite a lifelong passion for healthcare. Starting in general practice, I quickly immersed myself in the fast-paced environment, learning the intricacies of patient care and the healthcare system. Like many, I faced personal challenges—including a fear of needles—but I overcame this by training in phlebotomy at The Royal London Hospital. That decision laid the foundation for nearly 20 years of hands-on experience in phlebotomy and healthcare support.

Over the years, I have worked across a variety of clinical settings, gaining practical knowledge and developing a deep understanding of patient care. I have seen firsthand how confidence, skill, and support can transform a trainee’s experience, and this inspired me to share my expertise with others.

Today, as the founder of The Phlebotomy Trainer, I am passionate about helping individuals overcome their fears, build competence, and develop the practical skills needed for a career in healthcare. I provide structured training, guidance, and encouragement, focusing on phlebotomy and healthcare assistant skills, while creating a supportive environment for learning and growth. My goal is to empower trainees to feel confident, capable, and prepared as they take their first steps in the medical field.

What to Expect on Your First Training Day

Your first day is designed to give you a strong foundation in phlebotomy, combining essential theory with practical experience and preparing you for real-world patient care.

1. Introduction & Course Overview

The day begins with a warm welcome, covering ground rules, course objectives, and the role of a phlebotomist. You'll gain a clear understanding of what the profession entails and what will be expected of you throughout the course.

2. Core Theory Sessions

You'll explore the key procedures and protocols, infection control, health and safety, potential complications, and proper equipment handling. Throughout these sessions, there will be time for questions, ensuring you fully understand the principles before moving on to practice.

3. Awareness & Professional Responsibility

You'll develop an awareness of professional standards, patient care expectations, and the importance of clear communication, helping you feel confident in your role as a phlebotomist.

4. Practical Session on a Dummy Arm

After theory, you'll have the opportunity to practice your skills on a mannequin. This hands-on session allows you to master basic techniques in a safe, controlled environment, building confidence before working with real patients.

5. Preparation for Real-World Practice

During the Q&A, your trainer will explain what to expect in the next stage of your training: practical sessions at a GP practice with real patients. You'll learn about patient interaction, proper hygiene and safety, and how to apply the techniques you practiced on the dummy arm in a professional clinical setting. This ensures you are fully prepared to transition smoothly into patient care while maintaining confidence and competence.

By the end of the day, you'll have a solid understanding of phlebotomy fundamentals, hands-on practice experience, and a clear roadmap for the next step of your journey—working with real patients and continuing to build your skills in a real NHS environment.

GP Practice Practical Session

Once you've mastered the basics, you'll move on to your practical session at a GP practice. Here, you'll apply your skills under the guidance of your trainer with real patients, experiencing firsthand the day-to-day work of a phlebotomist in the NHS.

Before starting, you'll complete a confidentiality form, ensuring patient privacy and data protection. You'll also receive a brief introduction to the EMIS system, the electronic patient record system widely used in GP practices. During the session, you'll learn:

  • How to communicate professionally and put patients at ease
  • Proper hygiene, infection control, and safe handling of equipment
  • How to perform blood collection accurately and confidently
  • How to manage minor complications and respond calmly in unexpected situations

This session is designed to provide real-world insight, build confidence in a safe and supportive setting, and offer practical experience within a professional NHS context, helping you take the next step on your phlebotomy journey.
